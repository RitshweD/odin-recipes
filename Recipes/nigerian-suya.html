Nigerian Suya Recipe



Suya is a popular Nigerian street food made from skewered, spicy grilled meat.
Here’s a simple recipe to make it at home.

Ingredients
  • 1 lb (450 g) beef sirloin or chicken breast, cut into thin strips
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 2 tablespoons Suya spice mix (see below for a simple mix recipe)
  • 1 teaspoon ground paprika
  • 1 teaspoon ground cayenne pepper (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • Salt to taste
For the Suya Spice Mix:
  • 1/4 cup ground peanuts (roasted and unsalted)
  • 1 tablespoon paprika
  • 1 tablespoon ground ginger
  • 1 tablespoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt

Here we go. let the magic begin...

steps:

  1. Prepare the Suya Spice Mix:
    • In a bowl, mix all the spice mix ingredients together. You can
      store extra spice mix in an airtight container for future use.
  2. Marinate the Meat:
    • In a large bowl, combine the meat strips with the vegetable oil, Suya
      spice mix, paprika, cayenne pepper, garlic powder, onion powder, and
      salt. Mix well to ensure the meat is evenly coated.
    • Cover the bowl and marinate in the refrigerator for at least 1 hour,
      or overnight for best results.
  3. Preheat the Grill:
    • Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. If you’re using a charcoal grill,
      let the coals burn down until they’re covered with white ash.
  4. Skewer the Meat:
    • Thread the marinated meat strips onto skewers. If you’re using wooden skewers,
      soak them in water for 30 minutes before threading to prevent burning.
  5. Grill the Suya:
    • Place the skewers on the grill and cook for 4-5 minutes on each side, or until
      the meat is nicely charred and cooked through. Adjust cooking time based on
      the thickness of the meat strips.
  6. Serve:
    • Serve the Suya hot off the grill with sliced onions, tomatoes, and spicy pepper
      sauce. You can also serve it with flatbread or as part of a larger meal.
