Koko (Millet porridge) Recipe





Koko, also known as millet porridge, is a popular breakfast dish in Nigeria and other parts of West Africa.
It's simple to make and can be enjoyed with a variety of accompaniments.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup millet flour
  • 4 cups water (divided)
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground ginger (optional)
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cloves (optional)
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon (optional)
  • Sugar or honey to taste
  • Milk (optional)

Now here comes the magic...

Steps

  1. Mix the Millet Flour:
    • In a bowl, mix the millet flour with 1 cup of water. Stir until the mixture is smooth and
      free of lumps.
  2. Boil the Water:
    • In a medium-sized pot, bring the remaining 3 cups of water to a boil.
  3. Cook the Porridge:
    • Once the water is boiling, reduce the heat to medium and slowly pour in the millet mixture
      while stirring continuously to avoid lumps.
    • Add the ground ginger, cloves, and cinnamon (if using) to the pot and stir well.
    • Continue to cook the porridge, stirring frequently, for about 10-15 minutes or until it
      thickens to your desired consistency.
  4. Cook the Rice:
    • Add the rinsed rice to the pot, stir well to ensure the rice is evenly coated with the tomato mixture.
    • Reduce the heat to low, cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid or aluminum foil, and let the rice cook for
      about 20-30 minutes. Avoid opening the pot frequently to keep the steam inside.
  5. Sweeten and Serve:
    • Once the porridge is cooked, remove it from the heat and sweeten with sugar or honey to taste. If you prefer
      a creamier texture, you can stir in some milk.
    • Serve hot in bowls. You can enjoy it on its own or with sides like akara (bean cakes), bread, or puff-puff
      (fried dough balls).
